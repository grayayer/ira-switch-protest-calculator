# 03 — User Journey

## Entry Point

User arrives via a shared link — social media, email, or a blog post.

They are already emotionally primed: they've read something that made them want to act, and they followed a link to see if this is actually feasible.

---

## First Impression (0–5 seconds)

- They see the headline. It should be immediate and confrontational — not a product name, but a **statement**.
- They understand instantly what this tool does and why it exists.
- They see the privacy disclaimer and feel safe entering financial information.

---

## Orientation (5–15 seconds)

- They scan the inputs. There are only **five fields**. None of them are intimidating.
- They recognize that this is not a complex tax form — it's a focused question with a focused answer.

---

## Engagement (15–60 seconds)

They fill in their information:

1. They enter their recharacterization amount — probably $7,000
2. They select their filing status
3. They look up or estimate their taxable income and enter it
   - The app immediately shows them their **marginal rate** — this is often a small revelation; many people don't know their actual marginal rate
4. They enter their expected withdrawal year — they have to think about this one for a moment
5. They accept the default rate of return or adjust it

---

## Payoff (Immediate, Live)

- As they fill in the form, the output panel updates in **real time**
- By the time they enter their last field, the result is already there
- The **"net cost of the protest"** number is the focal point — the app surfaces it prominently

---

## Decision Point

They look at the number. Maybe it's $800. Maybe it's $3,200.

The app doesn't tell them what to do — it just tells them what it costs.

The copy around the result reframes this not as a loss but as the **price of a meaningful action**.

---

## Exit Paths

- They share the link with a partner or friend
- They go ahead with the recharacterization at their custodian
- They decide the cost is too high and close the tab
- They adjust the inputs to model different scenarios

---

## Failure States

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|----------|
| User doesn't know their taxable income | Helper text guides them |
| User enters an implausible withdrawal year (before current year) | Inline validation with a clear message |
| User enters zero or a negative amount | Inline validation |
